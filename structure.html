Our Current House Designs this 2024!

Traditonal House - In a traditional house design, one often encounters a harmonious blend of historical aesthetics and practicality. These homes typically feature steeply pitched roofs, echoing the architectural styles of bygone eras, be it Colonial, Tudor, or Craftsman. The facade may boast elements such as dormer windows, decorative trims, and perhaps even a welcoming porch adorned with intricate railings. Symmetry often plays a key role, with evenly spaced windows framing views of the surrounding landscape. Inside, expect cozy hearths, wooden beams stretching across ceilings, and warm, inviting spaces that encourage familial gatherings. Every detail, from the choice of materials to the layout of rooms, pays homage to tradition while accommodating modern living needs.


Classical House - In the classical house design, elegance and symmetry reign supreme, drawing inspiration from the architectural grandeur of ancient civilizations, particularly Greco-Roman influences. These homes typically showcase a sense of balance and proportion, with stately facades adorned with columns, pediments, and ornate cornices. The exterior may feature a grand entrance framed by towering columns, leading into spacious rooms adorned with marble floors, elaborate moldings, and soaring ceilings. Large windows with intricate grilles filter natural light into gracefully appointed interiors, where each room is meticulously designed to exude timeless sophistication. Whether it's a majestic manor or a refined villa, classical house design embodies a sense of timeless grace and enduring beauty.


Modern House - In modern house design, clean lines, open spaces, and functionality take center stage, reflecting the contemporary lifestyle's ethos. These homes often feature minimalist exteriors with flat or low-pitched roofs, large windows, and geometric shapes that emphasize simplicity and efficiency. The use of materials like concrete, steel, and glass creates a sleek aesthetic, while innovative design elements such as cantilevered structures or asymmetrical facades add visual interest. Inside, open floor plans promote fluidity and connectivity between living spaces, blurring the boundaries between indoors and outdoors. Natural light floods through expansive windows, illuminating interiors adorned with minimalist furnishings, neutral color palettes, and sleek finishes. Modern house design prioritizes efficiency, comfort, and a seamless integration of form and function, offering a contemporary sanctuary for modern living.
